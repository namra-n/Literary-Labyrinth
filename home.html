Top Picks Of The Day!

The Midnight Library by Matt Haig

"The Midnight Library" by Matt Haig is a thought-provoking and heartwarming novel that follows the story of Nora Seed, a woman who finds herself in a mysterious library between life and death. In this library, each book on the shelves represents a different version of her life, showcasing the various choices she could have made and the paths she could have taken.

The Book Thief by Markus Zusak

"The Book Thief" by Markus Zusak is a poignant novel set in Nazi Germany, narrated by Death. It follows the life of Liesel Meminger, a young girl living with a foster family, who discovers solace and escape through stealing books. As she navigates the challenges of her time, Liesel forms deep connections and experiences the transformative power of words.

Diary of a Young Girl by Anne Frank

"Diary of a Young Girl" is the poignant and powerful diary of Anne Frank, a Jewish girl in hiding during the Holocaust. Written between 1942 and 1944, Anne's diary captures her thoughts, fears, and dreams while living in hiding with her family. The diary provides an intimate perspective on the challenges of life in hiding and the profound impact of war on an individual's hopes and aspirations.

The Theory Of Everything by Stephen Hawking

The book the "theory of everything" is written by Stephen Hawking. This book is mostly about Cosmology. This book is comprised of seven lectures, these lectures are on the creation and the fate of the universe, Black holes, direction of time, and the theory of everything
